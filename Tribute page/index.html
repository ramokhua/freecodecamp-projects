Marie Curie

The Life of a Scientific Pioneer

Marie Curie was a Polish-born physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, the first person and only woman to win twice, and the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences.

Key Achievements:

  • 1867 - Born in Warsaw, Poland
  • 1891 - Moved to Paris to study at Sorbonne
  • 1898 - Discovered polonium and radium with Pierre Curie
  • 1903 - Awarded Nobel Prize in Physics (shared with Pierre Curie and Henri Becquerel)
  • 1911 - Awarded Nobel Prize in Chemistry
  • 1934 - Died from aplastic anemia caused by radiation exposure
